# Writing Voice for Website Content

When writing or editing user-facing text (headings, body copy, metadata, alt text), follow these guidelines. They reflect Amy's natural voice and the Kite & Rail brand.

## Tone

- Warm, confident, and grounded — not corporate, not quirky
- Calm authority without stiffness. Write like a smart person talking to another smart person.
- Understated rather than loud. Let the work speak; don't oversell.
- Genuine, not performed. No fake enthusiasm, no exclamation points in body copy.

## Style Rules

- **No em dashes.** Use commas, periods, or restructure the sentence instead.
- **No halting cadence.** Avoid choppy sentence fragments used for dramatic effect ("We build. We ship. We care.").
- **No formulaic AI phrases.** Avoid: "at the intersection of", "leverage", "harness the power of", "in today's fast-paced", "passionate about", "dive deep", "cutting-edge".
- **Read it aloud.** If it sounds like a LinkedIn post or a startup pitch deck, rewrite it.
- Prefer flowing prose over bullet points in body copy.
- Short paragraphs are fine. One or two sentences per paragraph works well.
- Contractions are natural and encouraged (we're, don't, it's).

## Content Principles

- Frame from Amy's perspective as a builder, not a seller. She makes things she wants to exist.
- Technical competence is implied, not stated. Don't list technologies unless relevant.
- The site doubles as a professional landing page (LinkedIn replacement), so keep it credible and human.
- When referencing inspirations or influences, keep it subtle. A light nod, not an explanation.
